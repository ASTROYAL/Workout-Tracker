📌 Calf Raise Progression Guide
Weeks 1–2: Bodyweight only. Master the full ROM — deep stretch at bottom, full rise at top, 1s pause. Most people skip the stretch and wonder why calves never grow.
Week 3 onwards: Add load — use the calf raise machine, hold a dumbbell in one hand, or use the Smith machine. Add 5kg whenever 4×20 feels easy.
Why calves need weight: You walk on them all day — they're adapted to bodyweight. They only grow under progressive overload. Treat them like any other muscle.

04 Fat Loss Protocol

HIIT — your
fat burning engine.

20 minutes of HIIT beats 60 minutes of jogging for fat loss. It creates an afterburn effect (EPOC) where your body burns extra calories for 12–24 hours after the session.

The rule: Always lift first. Do HIIT after. Lifting + HIIT on the same day = maximum fat burning while preserving muscle.

05 Progressive Overload

Get stronger
every week.

Weeks 1–2
Foundation
Start at 60–70% of your estimated max. Focus entirely on form. No ego lifting.
Weeks 3–6
Build Phase
Add 2.5 kg to every compound lift you hit all reps on. Add 1 rep to accessories.
Every 4th Week
Deload
Drop all weights to 60%. Same exercises, same sets, same reps — just lighter. Non-optional.
Month 2+
Periodisation
Alternate 3–5 rep strength blocks and 8–12 rep hypertrophy blocks every 4 weeks.
06 Lifestyle Optimisation

The things
outside the gym.

😴
Sleep — Your Biggest Variable
You're sleeping 6–7 hrs which is below optimal. Testosterone and growth hormone peak at 7–9 hrs. Going from 6.5 to 7.5 hrs will visibly improve results in 2–3 weeks. In bed by 10:30 PM.
💧
Hydration
Drink 3–3.5 litres daily. 500ml on waking, 500ml pre-workout, 500ml during training. Add a pinch of salt on training days. Chaas counts as hydration and protein.

Weigh every morning after bathroom, before eating. Take the weekly average. Scale may not move for 2–3 weeks on recomp — judge by strength gains and mirror.

High stress = stored belly fat. 10-min walk after dinner. 5 min deep breathing before bed. Sunday is sacred — full rest, no gym.

08 Exercise Form Guides

How to do
every lift right.

Read the form guide for the day's exercises before you go to the gym. Then do one warm-up set with very light weight just practising the movement before loading up.

Barbell Bench Press
Push · Chest, Shoulders, Triceps
Setup
  • Eyes directly under the bar
  • Grip slightly wider than shoulders
  • Pinch shoulder blades together and down
  • Feet flat on the floor, drive through heels
  • Small natural arch in lower back
Execution
  • Lower slowly (3s) to your lower chest
  • Touch chest lightly — don't bounce
  • Press up and slightly back toward rack
  • Lock out elbows at top, squeeze chest
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Chest stretch at bottom, squeeze at top
  • ✗ Don't flare elbows 90° — keep 45–75°
  • ✗ Don't bounce bar off chest
  • ✗ Don't lift your butt off bench
Overhead Press (Barbell)
Push · Shoulders, Triceps
Setup
  • Feet shoulder-width, grip just outside shoulders
  • Bar rests on upper chest / front delts
  • Brace core like you expect a punch
  • Squeeze glutes — keeps lower back safe
Execution
  • Press straight up — vertical bar path
  • Move head back slightly as bar passes face
  • Lock out arms fully, shrug traps up at top
  • Lower slowly and repeat
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Front delts firing, core staying tight
  • ✗ Don't lean back excessively
  • ✗ Don't press forward — go straight up
  • ✗ Don't use leg drive
Pull-Up
Pull · Lats, Biceps, Upper Back
Setup
  • Overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulders
  • Start from a dead hang — arms fully extended
  • Pull shoulder blades down and back first
  • Slight forward lean at the bottom
Execution
  • Think "pull elbows to hips" not "pull hands up"
  • Drive chest up toward the bar
  • Chin clears the bar = one rep
  • Lower slowly (3s) to full dead hang
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Feel lats (sides of back) — not just arms
  • ✗ Don't kip or use momentum
  • ✗ Don't half-rep — full hang every time
  • ✗ Can't do one yet? Use lat pulldown first
Seated Cable Row
Pull · Mid-Back, Lats, Traps
Setup
  • Sit tall, feet on the platform
  • Slight forward lean to grab handle
  • Chest up, back flat before you pull
  • Use a V-handle or wide bar
Execution
  • Pull handle to your belly button
  • Lead with elbows — drive them back
  • Squeeze shoulder blades hard at the end
  • Return slowly, feel the stretch
  • Don't rock forward and back
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Squeeze between shoulder blades at end
  • ✗ Don't use body momentum to row
  • ✗ Don't shrug traps up
  • ✗ Don't let lower back round
Barbell Back Squat
Legs · Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core
Setup
  • Bar on upper traps, grip to pull it in tight
  • Feet shoulder-width, toes out 15–30°
  • Big breath into belly, brace core hard
  • Unrack and take 2 steps back only
Execution
  • Push knees out toward toes
  • Descend like sitting back into a chair
  • Break parallel — hip crease below knee
  • Drive through the whole foot to stand
  • Keep chest up throughout
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Quads and glutes working equally
  • ✗ Don't let knees cave inward
  • ✗ Don't squat to just parallel — go below
  • ✗ Don't rise on your toes
Conventional Deadlift
Pull Day · Full Posterior Chain — Most Important Lift
Setup — Spend Time Here
  • Feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot
  • Hinge down, grip just outside legs
  • Shins vertical, hips above knees
  • Chest up, back flat — "proud chest"
  • Big breath, brace core maximally
Execution
  • "Push the floor away" — not "pull up"
  • Bar drags up your shins and thighs
  • Hips and shoulders rise at the same rate
  • Lock out: stand tall, squeeze glutes hard
  • Hinge back down — don't drop the bar
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Hamstrings and glutes loading, back tight
  • ✗ Don't round your lower back — ever
  • ✗ Don't let bar drift away from body
  • ✗ Don't jerk — build tension first, then pull
Sumo Deadlift
Legs B · Glutes, Inner Thighs, Hamstrings
Setup
  • Wide stance — feet near the plates
  • Toes pointed out ~45°
  • Grip the bar inside your legs, narrow grip
  • Hips lower than conventional — more squat-like
  • Chest up, shins nearly vertical
Execution
  • Push knees out hard as you pull
  • Drive through heels, not toes
  • Bar stays close to body — drags up inner thighs
  • Lock out by squeezing glutes — stand tall
  • Lower with control, reset each rep
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Glutes and inner thighs doing the work
  • ✗ Don't let knees cave in — push them out
  • ✗ Don't round lower back
  • ✗ Don't let hips shoot up at the start
Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
Legs · Hamstrings, Glutes
Setup
  • Start standing, bar at hip level
  • Feet hip-width, soft bend in knees
  • Shoulder blades back and down
  • This is a hinge — move hips back
Execution
  • Push hips back — bar drags down thighs
  • Lower until you feel a deep hamstring stretch
  • Stop at your flexibility — don't force it
  • Drive hips forward to stand, squeeze glutes
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Deep stretch in back of thighs
  • ✗ Don't bend knees too much — becomes a squat
  • ✗ Don't round lower back to reach lower
  • ✗ Don't let bar drift away from legs
Bulgarian Split Squat
Legs · Quads, Glutes — Start Bodyweight Only
Setup
  • Rear foot elevated on bench, laces down
  • Front foot far enough that shin stays vertical
  • Start with hands on hips — learn balance first
  • Stand tall, core braced
Execution
  • Lower straight down — don't lunge forward
  • Front knee tracks over toes, not caving in
  • Rear knee drops toward floor
  • Drive through front heel to return
  • All reps one side, then switch
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Front quad and glute doing the work
  • ✗ Don't lean too far forward
  • ✗ Don't add weight until balance is solid
  • ✗ Bodyweight only for the first 2 weeks
Cable Face Pull
Pull · Rear Delts, Rotator Cuff — Injury Prevention
Setup
  • Cable at face height or above
  • Rope attachment, thumbs pointing toward you
  • Step back so cable is taut with arms extended
  • Light weight — this is a health exercise
Execution
  • Pull rope toward forehead, splitting it apart
  • Elbows flare out to sides at shoulder height
  • At the end: externally rotate — hands by ears
  • Hold 1 second, squeeze rear delts
  • Return slowly, repeat
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Back of shoulder (rear delt) squeezing
  • ✗ Don't pull to your chin — aim for forehead
  • ✗ Don't use heavy weight or momentum
  • ✗ Don't skip — protects shoulder health
Bicep Curl Machine
Pull · Biceps — Strict Isolation
Setup
  • Adjust seat so upper arms rest flat on pad
  • Elbows aligned with the machine's pivot point
  • Grip the handles — palms facing up
  • Sit tall, no slouching
Execution
  • Curl all the way up — full contraction
  • Squeeze hard for 1 second at the top
  • Lower slowly over 3 full seconds
  • Arms fully extended at the bottom — full stretch
  • No momentum — the pad locks you in
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Bicep burning — full squeeze at top
  • ✗ Don't let elbows lift off the pad
  • ✗ Don't rush the negative — 3s down always
  • ✗ Don't use a weight you can't fully control
Dumbbell Lateral Raise
Push · Side Deltoids — Shoulder Width
Setup
  • Stand, dumbbells at sides, palms inward
  • Slight forward lean (10–15°) — hits side delt more
  • Soft bend in elbows
  • Go lighter than you think you need
Execution
  • Raise arms out to sides — like pouring water
  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • Stop when arms are parallel to floor (90°)
  • Lower slowly over 3 seconds
  • Don't rest at the bottom
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Burning on outer/top of shoulder
  • ✗ Don't shrug traps up
  • ✗ Don't swing or use momentum
  • ✗ Don't raise above parallel
Pec Deck / Pec Fly Machine
Push · Chest Isolation — Constant Tension
Setup
  • Adjust seat so handles are at chest height
  • Sit tall, back firmly against the pad
  • Grip handles, elbows slightly bent
  • Chest up, shoulders pulled back before starting
Execution
  • Bring handles together in a hugging arc
  • Squeeze chest hard when handles meet
  • Hold the squeeze for 1 full second
  • Open back slowly (3s) — feel the stretch
  • Don't slam the weight stack at the back
Feel + Mistakes
  • ✓ Chest squeeze at centre, stretch at widest
  • ✗ Don't let shoulders roll forward
  • ✗ Don't use momentum — controlled always
  • ✗ Don't go so wide it stresses the shoulder
Universal Form Checklist — Apply to Every Single Exercise
  • ✓ Core braced before every rep
  • ✓ Controlled descent (2–3 sec down)
  • ✓ Full range of motion — no half reps
  • ✓ Exhale on effort (the hard part)
  • ✓ If you can't feel the target muscle, reduce weight
  • ✗ Never train through joint pain
  • ✗ Never add weight if form breaks down
  • ✗ Never skip the warm-up set on compounds
  • ✗ Never hold your breath on heavy sets
  • ✗ Never rush — speed is the enemy of gains
